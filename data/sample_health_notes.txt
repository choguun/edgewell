Sleep
- Most adults need 7 to 9 hours of sleep per night.
- A consistent bedtime and wake time improve mood and energy.
- Avoid screens 30 minutes before bed; it shortens sleep onset.

Movement
- Walking 8000 steps per day is associated with lower mortality.
- Two short strength sessions per week preserve muscle mass past 40.
- Stretching the hip flexors and chest helps desk workers.

Nutrition
- Hydration: 2 liters of water a day supports kidney function.
- A high-protein breakfast reduces afternoon cravings.
- Aim for 5 servings of vegetables and fruit a day.
- Limit added sugar to under 25 grams per day.

Stress
- Box breathing (4-4-4-4) drops acute stress in under two minutes.
- A 10-minute walk after lunch improves digestion and mood.
- Talking to a friend is one of the most effective stress busters.

Money
- The 50/30/20 budget is a sane default: 50% needs, 30% wants, 20% savings.
- Build a 6-month emergency fund before investing aggressively.
- Track every expense for at least 30 days; most people are surprised.

Safety
- If you experience chest pain, sudden weakness on one side, severe
  bleeding, or thoughts of self-harm, seek immediate medical help.
