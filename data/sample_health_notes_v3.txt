EdgeWell v3.0.0 sample health notes
====================================

This file is meant to be ingested with `edgewell rag ingest
sample_health_notes_v3.txt` to give the v3.0.0 vector RAG something
to work with. The chunks are short and self-contained so the
hybrid search demo runs end to end in a few seconds.

Sleep
-----

A consistent bedtime is the single biggest lever for sleep quality.
Going to bed within a 30-minute window each night trains the
circadian rhythm and reduces the time it takes to fall asleep.
Caffeine has a half-life of about five hours, so a 14:00 coffee is
still half as strong at 19:00 and a quarter as strong at midnight.
Alcohol helps people fall asleep faster but it fragments REM
sleep, leaving you less restored in the morning.

Movement
--------

A daily walk of at least 30 minutes at a pace that raises your
heart rate but still lets you hold a conversation is associated
with a 20% lower all-cause mortality in observational studies.
Strength training twice a week protects bone density and joint
health as we age. Mobility work — gentle stretching, yoga, or
foam rolling — reduces injury risk and improves recovery.

Nutrition
---------

A balanced plate is roughly half vegetables, a quarter lean
protein, and a quarter complex carbohydrates. The Mediterranean
diet pattern — olive oil, fish, nuts, whole grains — has the
strongest evidence base for cardiovascular and cognitive health.
Ultra-processed foods are linked to weight gain independent of
calorie count, possibly because of how they are engineered to
override satiety signals.

Hydration
---------

Two litres of water a day is a reasonable adult target, more in
hot climates or during exercise. Thirst is a lagging signal — by
the time you feel thirsty, you are already 1-2% dehydrated. A
reusable bottle on the desk is the simplest hydration intervention.

Stress
------

Box breathing — inhale 4s, hold 4s, exhale 4s, hold 4s — is a
fast, free, evidence-based way to bring the autonomic nervous
system back toward baseline. Daily 10-minute sessions measurably
lower cortisol in a few weeks. Sleep, movement, and social
connection are the three pillars of chronic stress resilience.

Finances
--------

A simple monthly budget — income minus fixed costs minus savings
goal — surfaces where the discretionary dollars go. Automating
the savings transfer the day after payday removes willpower
from the equation. An emergency fund of three to six months of
expenses is the single biggest defence against debt spirals.
